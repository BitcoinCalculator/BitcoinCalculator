The Model

Why the Power Law Has Held for 15 Years

Most price models fail because they mistake correlation for causation. They draw a line through a chart and call it a prediction. Giovanni Santostasi's Power Law is different. It is grounded in a structural observation about how Bitcoin adoption grows — the same mathematical pattern that governs cities, biological organisms, the internet, and virtually every complex network that humans have ever built.

Power law relationships appear throughout nature and society: the size distribution of cities, the frequency of words in language, earthquake magnitudes, and stock market returns all follow power laws. The key insight is that as a system scales, certain properties grow not linearly but by a constant exponent. Bitcoin's price has grown by roughly the same exponent relative to time since the Genesis Block — and it has done so across four complete market cycles, including crashes of 80% or more.

"Bitcoin is not a bubble — it is a power law. The price oscillates around a rising baseline that compounds over decades, not years."

— Giovanni Santostasi, PhD, physicist & Bitcoin researcher

The model defines two corridor bounds. The upper resistance line represents the historic ceiling of bull market peaks — every major cycle top has touched this line and then reverted. The lower support line represents the historic floor — every major bear market bottom has touched or approached this line before recovering. Between the two corridors, the midline represents the "fair value" at any moment in time.

Power Law formula: Price ≈ 10^(5.84 × log₁₀(days_since_genesis) − 17.01) Upper: multiply by ~10x | Lower: divide by ~10x

What makes the model extraordinary is not that it predicted specific prices — it did not. What it predicted is the range. Bitcoin has never spent a meaningful period of time outside the corridor. Every time it approached the upper bound, it eventually pulled back. Every time it collapsed toward the lower bound, it eventually recovered. The corridor contracts in percentage terms over time as Bitcoin matures, meaning the volatility decreases as the market deepens.
